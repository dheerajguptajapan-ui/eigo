2. Product Overview
2.1 The Japanese Learner Problem
Japanese speakers face a uniquely difficult path to English fluency, driven by structural factors that generic apps ignore:
Script gap: Japanese uses three scripts (Hiragana, Katakana, Kanji) â€” the Roman alphabet requires explicit training
Sound system mismatch: Japanese has ~100 phonemes vs English's ~44. Sounds like L/R, V/B, TH, and consonant clusters do not exist in Japanese, causing persistent pronunciation errors
Grammar inversion: Japanese is SOV (Subject-Object-Verb); English is SVO. Sentence construction requires mental rewiring
Katakana interference: Loan words absorbed into Japanese as ã‚«ã‚¿ã‚«ãƒŠ (e.g., ãƒžã‚¯ãƒ‰ãƒŠãƒ«ãƒ‰ for McDonald's) create false pronunciation confidence
Cultural hesitancy: Japanese communication norms favor silence and indirectness â€” spoken practice causes anxiety in learners
Eigo Master addresses every one of these barriers by design.
